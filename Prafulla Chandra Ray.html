BIOGRAPHY

Sir Acharya Prafulla Chandra Ray, (CIE, FNI, FRASB, FIAS, FCS) was an Indian chemist, educationist, historian, industrialist and philanthropist. He established the first modern Indian research school in chemistry (post classical age) and is regarded as the Father of Indian Chemistry.

The Royal Society of Chemistry honoured his life and work with the first ever Chemical Landmark Plaque outside Europe. He was the founder of Bengal Chemicals & Pharmaceuticals, India's first pharmaceutical company. He is the author of A History of Hindu Chemistry from the Earliest Times to the Middle of the Sixteenth Century (1902).

1. Birth and Family:
- He was born on August 2, 1861, in Raruli-Katipara, a village in present-day Bangladesh, which was part of British India at the time. His family was relatively modest in means.

2. Schooling:
- Ray began his early education at the Hare School and later attended the Albert School in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India.

3. College and University:
- He graduated with honors in chemistry from the University of Calcutta. After completing his Bachelor of Science degree, he pursued further studies in chemistry.

4. Abroad for Higher Studies:
- In 1882, Prafulla Chandra Ray sailed to England to pursue higher studies in chemistry.
- He was one of the first Indians to do so.
- He gained admission to the University of Edinburgh and later to the University of Glasgow.

5. Ph.D. and Research:
- He earned his Doctor of Science (D.Sc.) degree from the University of Edinburgh in 1887.
- During his time in Europe, he conducted research on various topics in chemistry, gaining valuable experience.

6. Return to India:
- After completing his studies in Europe, Ray returned to India in 1888, carrying with him a wealth of knowledge and expertise in chemistry.

7. Teaching Career:
- He started his teaching career at the Presidency College, Calcutta, which is now known as Presidency University.
- He later became the first Indian to be appointed as a professor of chemistry at the University of Calcutta.

Prafulla Chandra Ray's early life and education laid the foundation for his remarkable career as a chemist and educator, making significant contributions to the field of chemistry and scientific education in India.

CAREER

Prafulla Chandra Ray had a distinguished career as a chemist, educator, and entrepreneur. Here are some key highlights of his career:

1. Teaching and Research:
- After returning to India from his studies in Europe, P. C. Ray began his teaching career at Presidency College, Calcutta (now Kolkata).
- He later became the first Indian to be appointed as a professor of chemistry at the University of Calcutta.

2. Founding Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works:
- One of his most significant contributions was the founding of the Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works (BCPW) in 1892.
- This company played a crucial role in the development of the chemical industry in India, producing a range of chemicals and pharmaceuticals.
- It was one of the earliest instances of indigenous pharmaceutical manufacturing in India.

3. Research Contributions:
- Ray conducted extensive research in the field of chemistry.
- His work included the isolation of various chemical compounds, studies on mercurous nitrate, nitrites, and ammonium nitrite.
- His research contributions were published in scientific journals.

4. Promotion of Science:
- He actively promoted scientific education and research in India.
- His efforts in establishing the University of Calcutta's first chemistry department and founding the Indian Chemical Society were instrumental in advancing the field of chemistry in India.

5. Awards and Recognition:
- Prafulla Chandra Ray received several accolades during his career, including being knighted by the British government in 1919.
- He was also elected as a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh.

6. Legacy:
- His legacy continues to influence the scientific community in India, particularly in the field of chemistry.
- He is remembered for his pioneering work in both chemical research and the promotion of scientific education and industry in India.

Prafulla Chandra Ray's career left an indelible mark on the development of chemistry and the chemical industry in India, and his contributions are celebrated to this day.

research and contributions

Prafulla Chandra Ray made significant research contributions in the field of chemistry, particularly in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. Some of his notable research and contributions include:
1. Chemical Isolation and Identification:
- Ray worked on the isolation and identification of several chemical compounds.
- One of his significant achievements was the isolation of mercurous nitrate and the determination of its chemical structure.

2. Stable Nitrites:
- He conducted groundbreaking research on nitrites and was known for his work on the stability of nitrites, including ammonium nitrite.
- His studies contributed to a better understanding of the chemistry of these compounds.

3. Chemical Kinetics:
- Ray made contributions to the field of chemical kinetics, particularly in the study of the rate of chemical reactions.
- His work in this area helped advance the understanding of reaction mechanisms.

4. Educational Contributions:
- In addition to his research, he played a crucial role in promoting scientific education in India.
- He established the University of Calcutta's first chemistry department, which became a center for chemical research and education.

5. Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works:
- Ray's entrepreneurial contribution was the founding of the Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works (BCPW).
- This company produced a wide range of chemicals and pharmaceuticals and played a pivotal role in the development of the indigenous chemical industry in India.

6. Indian Chemical Society:
- He founded the Indian Chemical Society in 1924 to promote the advancement of chemical science in India.
- This society continues to support and foster research in the chemical sciences.

Prafulla Chandra Ray's research and contributions left a lasting impact on the field of chemistry in India. His work not only advanced chemical knowledge but also paved the way for the growth of the chemical industry and scientific education in the country.

DEATH

Prafulla Chandra Ray passed away on June 16, 1944. His death marked the end of an era in Indian chemistry. Despite his passing, his legacy continues to live on through his significant contributions to the field of chemistry, the establishment of the Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works, and his efforts to promote scientific education and research in India. Ray's contributions continue to be celebrated and recognized in the scientific community, and he is remembered as one of India's pioneering chemists.
